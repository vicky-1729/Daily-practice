## SLIDE 2 — SmartOps Deep Dive (4–5.5 min)

So, **what is SmartOps?**

SmartOps is a **Generative AI tool** built inside the WINGS platform. It helps manage IT incidents from start to finish. It does three things — it **sorts** incidents, it **adds useful information** to them, and in many cases, it **fixes them automatically**.

Let me explain each one.

**First — Sorting incidents, or Triage.**
When a new incident comes in, SmartOps reads it and understands what the problem is. Then it decides how important it is and sends it to the right team. Today, a person does this manually. SmartOps does it in seconds. This saves a lot of time, especially when there are many tickets coming in at once.

**Second — Adding information, or Enrichment.**
This is very important. When an engineer gets a ticket, the first thing they do is look for more information — what happened, when did it start, has this happened before, what do the logs say. This searching takes time. SmartOps does this automatically. It checks your monitoring tools, your past tickets, your knowledge base, and puts all the useful information right on the ticket. So when the engineer opens it, everything is already there. They can start working on the fix right away.

**Third — Fixing known problems, or Automated Remediation.**
Some problems happen again and again. We already know how to fix them. For these types of issues, SmartOps can run the fix on its own — no person needed. It finds the problem, runs the solution, and closes the ticket. This means our engineers do not have to spend time on problems that already have a known answer. They can work on new and harder problems instead.

Now, one more important thing — SmartOps works with the tools you already use. It connects with **ServiceNow**, your **monitoring tools**, and your **knowledge bases**. You do not need to change your current setup. SmartOps just makes it work better and faster.

To say it simply — SmartOps changes incident management from **"waiting for someone to fix it"** to **"it is already being fixed."**

And this is not just an idea. It is real. It is running in production today. We are already using it for our customers, and you are about to hear more from the people who are doing this work every day.
